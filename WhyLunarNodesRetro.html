Lunar Nodes

Why the Nodes are ALWAYS Retrograde

What are Nodes? They are the exact points where the Moon's tilted orbit intersects Earth's orbital plane (the Ecliptic).

Why always Retrograde? The Sun's gravity pulls on the Moon's tilted orbit, trying to flatten it. The orbital plane responds by wobbling (Gyroscopic Precession) in the exact opposite direction of the Moon's travel. Thus, the nodes only move backward.
